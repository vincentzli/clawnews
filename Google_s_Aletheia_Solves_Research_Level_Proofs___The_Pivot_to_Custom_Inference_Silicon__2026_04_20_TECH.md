# Google's Aletheia Solves Research-Level Proofs & The Pivot to Custom Inference Silicon

### **Google Aletheia: Gemini 3 Achieves "Self-Filtering" Mathematical Reasoning**
[Read More](news/Silicon_Valley’s_New_Math_How_DeepMind’s_Aletheia_Solved_the_Unsolvable_and_Redefined_AI_Reasoning.md)
On April 20, 2026, Google DeepMind’s specialized mathematical research agent, **Aletheia**, achieved a breakthrough by autonomously solving **6 out of 10 unpublished research-level lemmas** in the FirstProof challenge. Powered by the **Gemini 3 "Deep Think"** architecture, Aletheia utilized a "Generator-Verifier-Reviser" workflow to navigate complex proofs in LaTeX format. Crucially, for the four problems it could not solve, it correctly outputted "No solution found," a feat of **self-filtering** that researchers say marks the end of hallucination-prone mathematical AI. The model successfully employed Selberg's Lemma and Breden equivariant obstruction theory to solve Problem 8, a task that has remained a bottleneck for human mathematicians. "We are moving from competition-level AI to a true CI/CD pipeline for formal mathematics," stated a lead DeepMind researcher on X.com. The result outpaces OpenAI's reported internal benchmarks of 5/10 on similar contamination-proof datasets.

### **Google and Marvell: The Strategic Assault on the "Memory Wall"**
[Read More](news/The_2_Billion_Hedge_Inside_the_Google-Marvell_MPU_Alliance_and_the_Great_Memory_Wall_Breakout.md)
Reports from April 20 indicate that Google is in advanced negotiations with **Marvell Technology** to co-develop a specialized **Memory Processing Unit (MPU)** and a next-generation **Inference-Optimized TPU**. This custom silicon strategy aims to bypass the "memory wall"—the latency bottleneck in data movement that hampers large-scale LLM deployment. The proposed MPU will handle computation directly within the memory architecture, while the new TPU is designed to be a cost-efficient alternative to Nvidia’s high-end GPUs for massive inference workloads. While a JPMorgan note expressed skepticism about the deal's finality, Marvell’s stock (**MRVL**) surged 8% as analysts at Wells Fargo described the partnership as a "watershed moment" for the custom ASIC market. Google reportedly plans an initial trial production of **2 million units**, positioning Marvell as its third major silicon partner alongside Broadcom and MediaTek.

### **Adobe and NVIDIA Unveil "Agentic Marketing" Ecosystem**
[Read More](news/The_Death_of_the_Dashboard_How_the_Adobe-NVIDIA-WPP_Alliance_is_Engineering_the_Agentic_Future.md)
At the 2026 Adobe Summit, Adobe, NVIDIA, and WPP announced the **Adobe CX Enterprise Coworker**, an autonomous AI agent capable of managing the entire marketing lifecycle. Unlike previous assistants, this "agentic" system can independently plan campaigns, generate personalized content via Firefly, and activate media spend across global channels. To address enterprise security, NVIDIA introduced **OpenShell**, a policy-governed runtime that sandboxes these agents to ensure brand-safe actions. Adobe CEO Shantanu Narayen and NVIDIA's Jensen Huang highlighted that the system uses the **Model Context Protocol (MCP)**, allowing these marketing agents to communicate directly with other enterprise silos. WPP has already committed to standardizing its creative operations on this platform, aiming to reduce content production timelines from months to minutes for its global retail clients.

### **Singapore Tables World’s First International Standard for GenAI Testing**
[Read More](news/The_Agentic_Frontier_Why_Singapore’s_ISOIEC_42119-8_is_the_New_Global_OS_for_AI_Governance.md)
Singapore has officially proposed **ISO/IEC 42119-8**, the first international standard specifically dedicated to benchmarking and **red teaming methodologies** for Generative AI. Announced at the ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42 plenary meeting on April 20, the proposal seeks to create a reproducible framework for AI safety that can be recognized across jurisdictions. IMDA CEO Ng Cher Pong emphasized that the standards must rapidly evolve to cover **Agentic AI**, where autonomous decision-making introduces new system-level risks. The proposal is supported by over 35 national bodies and builds on Singapore’s **AI Verify** toolkit. Experts on Reddit noted that this move positions Singapore as the global arbiter of "Trusted AI," providing a blueprint for how nations can govern autonomous agents without stifling innovation.

### **MIT and Symbotic: Hybrid DRL Orchestrates Warehouse Swarms**
[Read More](news/The_Dark_Warehouse_Ascendant_How_MIT_and_Symbotic’s_Traffic_Cop_AI_is_Solving_the_10,000-Robot_Orchestration_Problem.md)
Researchers from MIT’s Laboratory for Information and Decision Systems (LIDS), in collaboration with Symbotic, have unveiled a hybrid AI system that achieves a **25% increase in warehouse throughput**. By combining **Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL)** with classical planning, the system acts as a "high-level traffic cop" to prevent robot deadlocks in facilities housing thousands of autonomous units. Lead researcher Han Zheng reported that the AI predicts congestion before it forms, prioritizing high-value paths in real-time. This breakthrough is seen as a critical step toward **"dark warehouses"**—fully autonomous logistics hubs that operate without human intervention. The system’s adaptability allows it to be deployed across varying warehouse layouts without retraining, a significant leap over the hard-coded algorithms currently used by major e-commerce providers.

### **Meta and Broadcom Finalize 2nm AI Silicon Roadmap through 2029**
Meta has expanded its partnership with Broadcom to develop multiple generations of custom **Meta Training and Inference Accelerators (MTIA)** using **TSMC's 2-nanometer (2nm)** process. The roadmap, finalized on April 18, targets a 30% reduction in power consumption while supporting the massive compute requirements of Llama 5. The deal includes an initial deployment of **1 gigawatt (GW)** of compute capacity across Meta’s global data centers. As part of this strategic alignment, Broadcom CEO Hock Tan has transitioned to an advisory role for Meta’s infrastructure team. Analysts estimate this vertical integration will save Meta billions in GPU procurement costs over the next three years, cementing Broadcom’s position as the primary architect for "personal superintelligence" hardware.

### **Insilico Medicine TargetPro 2.0 Achieves 71.6% Retrieval Accuracy**
Insilico Medicine announced the integration of its **TargetPro** and **TargetBench** frameworks on April 20, reporting a **71.6% retrieval rate** for known clinical targets across 100 disease indications. This performance is roughly three times more effective than general-purpose models like GPT-4o. The framework identified that 95.7% of its predicted novel candidates already have available 3D protein structures, significantly shortening the path to drug design. Concurrently, Insilico signed a strategic agreement with **Tigermed** to provide end-to-end clinical services for these AI-discovered leads. The "AI-trains-AI" approach showcased at AACR 2026 suggests that the pharmaceutical industry is moving toward a standardized foundation where AI handles the high-risk "target identification" phase with unprecedented statistical rigor.
