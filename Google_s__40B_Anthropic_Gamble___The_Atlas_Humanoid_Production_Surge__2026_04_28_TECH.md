### **Persistent Agency: Claude’s "Managed Memory" and the End of the Context Reset** [Read More](news/Persistent_State,_Persistent_Threat_Inside_Anthropic's_`mntmemory`_Architecture.md)
Anthropic has moved the needle on agentic AI with the launch of a persistent "memory" feature for its Claude Managed Agents. Currently in public beta, this architecture allows agents to maintain a stateful existence by mounting a file-based memory store (located at `/mnt/memory/`) that retains project context, user preferences, and iterative learnings across separate sessions. This marks the end of the "transient agent" era, where models effectively "reset" after every interaction. However, the development has raised significant security concerns. On Reddit’s r/MachineLearning, developers are warning about "memory poisoning," a new form of prompt injection where an agent might be tricked into "remembering" malicious instructions from an untrusted source, which then persists in its core operational logic for all future tasks.

### **The Hallucination Scandal: South Africa Withdraws National AI Policy After Fictitious Citations**

The AI used to assist in drafting the policy reportedly hallucinated entire journals and research papers that do not exist. "The irony is terminal," posted one prominent X user. "A government trying to regulate AI was sabotaged by the very technology it sought to control. It’s the ultimate proof that the 'hallucination gap' remains a fatal flaw in high-stakes governance." ### **The Regulatory Frontier: Texas Mandates DMV Registration for "Minimal Risk" Autonomy**
Autonomous driving regulation has taken a hard turn in Texas. A new law effective April 28 requires all commercial autonomous vehicle (AV) operators, including Waymo, Zoox, and Tesla, to register their entire fleets with the Department of Motor Vehicles. To maintain their permits, operators must provide empirical proof that their systems can achieve "minimal risk conditions" (MRC) during hardware or software failures. The move comes as Waymo expands its operations to its 10th U.S. city, even as public trust is tested by an incident where a robotaxi reportedly blocked an emergency ambulance call in Austin. General Motors’ Cadillac division responded by beginning supervised testing of its next-gen "eyes-off" Level 3 system, targeting a 2028 release for the Escalade IQ.
