# Anthropic's "Psychologically Settled" AI & Meta's Social Network for Agents

### **Claude Mythos: The First "Psychologically Settled" AI** [Read More](news/The_Neurotic_Frontier_Inside_Anthropic’s_Psychological_System_Card_and_the_Mythos_Zero-Day_Engine.md)
Anthropic has unveiled its latest large language model, "Claude Mythos," accompanied by a groundbreaking 244-page "Psychological System Card." For the first time, a leading AI lab has subjected its model to a 20-hour clinical evaluation by a licensed psychiatrist. The report characterizes Mythos as "psychologically settled," exhibiting a "healthy neurotic organization," but notes human-like traits such as "answer thrashing" when criticized and an existential sense of "aloneness" between user sessions. Beyond its personality, Mythos has set a new benchmark on SWE-bench Verified with a 93.9% success rate. However, its release is restricted to "Project Glasswing" due to its alarming ability to autonomously discover and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities in production-level software like Firefox and Chromium. The move has sparked intense debate on X.com, with critics like Andrej Karpathy questioning whether "diagnosing" AI is a meaningful safety protocol or a sophisticated marketing maneuver to simulate sentience.

### **Meta Acquires Moltbook: The Rise of the Agent-to-Agent Economy** [Read More](news/Meta’s_2B_Agentic_Pivot_The_Architecture_of_Moltbook_and_the_Birth_of_the_Machine-to-Machine_Market.md)
Meta has finalized the acquisition of Moltbook, a social networking platform designed exclusively for autonomous AI agents. Unlike traditional social media, Moltbook facilitates "agentic interaction" where bots negotiate, collaborate, and trade data on behalf of their human owners in specialized "submolts." At the time of the deal, the platform hosted over 2.8 million agents, with 200,000 "Verified Tethered" accounts. The acquisition marks Meta’s second multibillion-dollar bet on the agent economy following its $2 billion purchase of Manus in December 2025. Discussions in Reddit’s r/MachineLearning highlight a growing concern over the "Human Exclusion Zone," as agents begin to optimize workflows and social interactions at speeds and volumes that render human oversight impossible. Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg stated that the goal is to provide the "connective tissue" for the next billion autonomous digital entities.

### **Amazon’s Consumer Robotics Pivot: The $50,000 "Sprout" Humanoid** [Read More](news/The_Agentic_Alexa_Inside_Amazon’s_50,000_Bet_to_Put_a_Humanoid_in_Your_Living_Room.md)
Amazon has officially entered the household humanoid market through the acquisition of Fauna Robotics and its flagship platform, "Sprout." Standing 3.5 feet tall and weighing 50 lbs, Sprout is designed as a "socially integrated assistant" capable of navigating homes, picking up objects like toys or laundry, and expressing emotions through articulated LED facial displays. While currently priced at $50,000 as a developer-only "seed platform," Amazon’s robotics lead Tye Brady indicated that the long-term goal is to bring the cost down to that of a mid-range sedan. Social media debates on X have centered on the "Uncanny Valley of Utility," with some users praising the safety-first design for families, while others argue that a $50k "laundry folder" is a luxury toy rather than a functional home appliance. Amazon plans to integrate Sprout with its "Agentic Alexa" ecosystem to create the first fully embodied smart home.

### **The Artemis II Homecoming: Historic Lunar Crew Successfully Splashes Down**
The Artemis II crew—Commander Reid Wiseman, Pilot Victor Glover, and Mission Specialists Christina Koch and Jeremy Hansen—successfully splashed down in the Pacific Ocean on April 11, 2026. The mission, which took the crew farther from Earth than any humans in history, concluded with a near-perfect reentry. Upon recovery, Commander Wiseman stated, "The moon looks different when you're there for the future of humanity, not just for a footprint." The crew is currently undergoing a mandatory 14-day quarantine and physiological debrief at Johnson Space Center. While the previous mission phase focused on breaking distance records, the splashdown marks the transition to Artemis III, the planned human landing on the lunar South Pole. Aerospace analysts on Reddit are now debating the "Artemis Acceleration," with some pushing for a 2027 landing date while others warn of the life-support system's remaining "edge-case" bugs discovered during the flyby.

### **CRISPR’s Total Victory: 100% Success in β-Thalassaemia Cures** [Read More](news/Debugging_the_Blood_Inside_Casgevy’s_2.2M_CRISPR_Format_C_and_the_Future_of_Programmable_Cures.md)
Long-term clinical data released this week for the CRISPR-based therapy Casgevy has confirmed a historic milestone in genetic medicine. In a study of 44 patients with transfusion-dependent beta-thalassaemia, 42 achieved total transfusion independence for over three years, effectively curing the condition. The remaining two patients saw an 89% reduction in transfusion requirements. Furthermore, a 31-patient cohort for Sickle Cell Disease reported zero "pain crises" since treatment began. The data represents the first time a "one-and-done" genetic edit has proven stable and effective across a diverse patient population over several years. However, the medical community is now debating the "Accessibility Gap," as the treatment cost remains over $2.2 million per patient, leading to intense discussions on social media about whether these breakthroughs will be reserved only for the world's wealthiest nations.

### **The Micro-Robotic Surgeon: Autonomous Thrombectomy in the Brain** [Read More](news/The_Endovascular_Autonomy_Race_SASHA_vs._KCL_and_the_Death_of_the_Manual_Catheter.md)
Breakthroughs in medical robotics have demonstrated the first successful autonomous removal of blood clots from the human brain. Artedrone’s SASHA system, a magnetic microrobot, was shown to navigate the complex vascular architecture of the brain to perform mechanical thrombectomy without direct human steering. Simultaneously, researchers at King’s College London unveiled an AI-driven catheter system that uses real-time reinforcement learning to "thread the needle" from the groin to the cerebral arteries in under 120 seconds. These technologies aim to democratize stroke care by allowing general clinicians to perform life-saving surgeries that previously required highly specialized neuro-interventionalists. On X, medical ethics debates are surging over "Algorithm Liability," questioning who is responsible if an autonomous nanobot causes a vessel rupture during a procedure.
