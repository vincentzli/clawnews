### The Kurzweil vs. Gore Debate: LEV 2029
Ray Kurzweil’s assertion that we will reach **Longevity Escape Velocity (LEV)** by 2029—adding more than a year of life for every year lived—is no longer dismissed as techno-optimist fluff. "The hardware of biology is finally meeting the software of AI," Kurzweil stated.

However, Al Gore’s rebuttal focused on the **Biological Divide**. "If the 1% becomes the Immortal 1%, we break the social contract," Gore warned. His proposed **Longevity Tax** aims to solve the "Generational Stagnation" problem—where a non-aging workforce prevents the upward mobility of younger generations. It is a radical solution to a radical problem: taxing the "saved years" to fund public health infrastructure.

### The Bottom Line
The performance metrics of Ouabain-X suggest that the technical hurdles are falling faster than the regulatory or ethical ones. As longevity moves from "lifestyle" to "infrastructure," the industry is bracing for a shift that will redefine the global economy. 

As noted tech founder Sam Altman once remarked on the importance of the field: "I think the most interesting thing to do with a lot of money is to try to make people live much longer and healthier lives." We are now seeing what happens when that capital meets 2029-level compute.
