**The Architecture of the Leviathan: 3T MoE and the "Fine-Grained" Shift**
While Meta has not officially released a whitepaper, leaked specifications from infra-engineers suggest Muse Spark is a radical departure from the dense transformer architecture of the Llama 3 era. 

The model is a **Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)** behemoth featuring:
*   **Total Parameters:** 3.1 Trillion.
*   **Active Parameters:** ~180 Billion per forward pass.
*   **Expert Granularity:** A "Fine-Grained" routing system with 256 specialized experts, a 4x jump from the sparse architecture seen in earlier iterations.
*   **Training Density:** Trained on 42 Trillion tokens, utilizing Meta’s full 1.3-million GPU stockpile.

Technically, this isn't just a "larger Llama." It’s an implementation of **Efficiency Leverage (EL)** scaling laws. By using more, smaller experts, Meta has reportedly achieved a 65% reduction in "knowledge interference"—the phenomenon where general models forget niche reasoning when scaled. Muse Spark doesn't "know" everything at once; it routes queries to specialized sub-circuits, optimizing FLOPs for performance rather than brute-force density.

**The Economic Blitzkrieg: $0.05/1k Tokens**
The most disruptive element of Muse Spark isn't its reasoning—it’s its pricing. At **$0.05 per 1,000 tokens**, Meta is pricing Muse Spark 60% below the projected rates for OpenAI’s **GPT-5** and Anthropic’s **Claude 4**.

This is the "Efficiency Mandate" in action. Meta’s massive $135B CapEx in 2025 wasn't just about training; it was about vertical integration. By running Muse Spark on their in-house **MTIA v3** silicon, Meta has decoupled their inference costs from the "NVIDIA tax." 

"Zuck is running the Uber playbook on AI," says a prominent Silicon Valley VC. "They are using their balance sheet to subsidize the most powerful model on earth until every other proprietary API is squeezed out. But the cost of that subsidy is the end of the open Llama releases. You can't give away the crown jewels when the mine cost $100 billion."
