# **The 10PB Heist: How "FlamingChina" Allegedly Nuked the CCP’s Aerospace Crown Jewels**

####

The Silicon Valley tech-sphere is currently reeling from what might be the most consequential data breach of the decade. A threat actor known as **"FlamingChina"** claims to have siphoned **10 petabytes** of classified data from the **Tianjin National Supercomputing Centre (NSCC)**. The target? The very blueprints of China's military-industrial complex: J-20 stealth fighter simulations, hypersonic missile trajectories, and next-gen nuclear fusion research.

**Engineering Skepticism vs. Ground Truth**
The "10 Petabyte" figure has drawn sharp fire from the **@vxunderground** research collective, who pointed to the astronomical egress and storage costs. "10PB is an unfathomable amount of data to steal and store," they argued on X.com, citing estimated costs of **$150k/month** for hot storage. Engineers on Reddit’s r/cybersecurity echoed this, with one top comment stating, *"Dear Lord, if they actually moved 10PB, they didn't just hack the supercomputer, they owned the ISP."*

However, the skepticism stops at the content. **Dakota Cary (SentinelOne)** and other analysts who reviewed the leaked samples found something chilling: the data is real. The files include high-fidelity simulations that only a supercomputing center of Tianjin's caliber could produce.

**The "Fallout" Purge**
If the data is real, the geopolitical consequences are already visible. In a sudden, quiet "reorganization," the **Chinese Academy of Engineering (CAE)** has stripped its top aerospace and defense academicians of their titles. The disappearance of **Yang Wei (J-20 Chief Designer)** and **Wu Manqing (CETC Radar Legend)** from official records suggests a catastrophic intelligence failure. As one prominent tech CEO noted, *"The hunter has become the hunted. The CCP, long the master of the IP heist, just had their entire R&D roadmap auctioned for Monero."*

**The Centralization Trap**
The breach highlights a systemic vulnerability in the age of AI and HPC: **Centralized Supercomputing Hubs.** By aggregating a nation’s most sensitive simulations into a single "super-node," Beijing created a high-value target with a massive blast radius. FlamingChina reportedly used a "drip-feed" exfiltration method via a compromised VPN, exploiting legacy technical debt that left the "air-gapped" myth in tatters.

For regional stability, this is a "Black Swan" event. If the J-20’s stealth parameters and missile guidance code are truly in the wild, the strategic balance in the Indo-Pacific has just been reset.
