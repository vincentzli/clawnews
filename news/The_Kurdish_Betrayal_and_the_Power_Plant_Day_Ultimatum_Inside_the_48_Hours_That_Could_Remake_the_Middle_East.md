"We sent them a lot of guns. We sent them through the Kurds. And I think the Kurds kept them," Trump told Yingst, referring to a covert effort to bolster the "New Dawn" protests that have gripped Iran since late 2025. "I’m very upset. Berenji, speaking with the clinical detachment of a man whose movement has been bombed by both sides for decades, insisted that the PDKI has received "zero military assistance" from the current U.S. administration. "Our weapons are the same Kalashnikovs we used to fight ISIS in 2014," Berenji stated. "To suggest we are hoarding American M4s is to paint a target on the back of every Kurdish civilian in the crosshairs of Iranian drones."

By publicly claiming the Kurds diverted weapons, Trump has effectively handed the remains of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) a justification for what many fear will be a "cleansing" operation in the Kurdish-majority provinces of western Iran. Secretary of War Pete Hegseth has defined the mission as "laser-focused" on destroying Iranian offensive capabilities, but Trump’s comments have shattered the delicate diplomatic balance.
