# **The Stargate Gambit: Sam Altman’s $7 Trillion Shadow Empire and the Internal Revolt at OpenAI**

####

In the autumn of 2025, as the sun set over the Persian Gulf, a select group of OpenAI engineers found themselves aboard a $250 million superyacht owned by Sheikh Tahnoon bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the UAE’s national security adviser. The setting was as opulent as it was politically fraught. At the center of the gathering was Sam Altman, the man who has become the face of the artificial intelligence revolution. But as a comprehensive 18-month investigation by Ronan Farrow and Andrew Marantz in *The New Yorker* now reveals, the image Altman projects in Washington—a humble, safety-conscious visionary—is at stark odds with a private reality of aggressive lobbying, opaque foreign financing, and a management style described by his closest peers as "manipulative" and "deceptive."

The investigation, titled "Unmasking Sam Altman," draws on a trove of internal documents that pull back the curtain on the most powerful startup in history. Among the most critical is a 52-page memo compiled by Ilya Sutskever, OpenAI’s former chief scientist, just weeks before Altman’s brief ousting in late 2023. Sutskever, who delivered the document to independent board members via disappearing email for fear of retaliation, alleged a "consistent pattern of lying" and a habit of "pitting executives against one another." This was supported by over 200 pages of private notes from Dario Amodei, the Anthropic CEO who left OpenAI years earlier. Amodei’s notes detail how Altman allegedly "gaslit" colleagues and prioritized his $7 trillion infrastructure vision—code-named "Stargate"—over the very safety protocols he championed in front of Congress.

"Stargate" is no longer just a code name for a supercomputer; it has evolved into a $500 billion joint venture known as Stargate LLC. Backed by Masayoshi Son’s SoftBank and the UAE’s investment firm MGX, the project aims to overhaul the global semiconductor industry. However, the scale of the ambition has triggered a firestorm in Washington. Under the current administration of President Donald Trump—who took office after Joe Biden’s departure—the Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States (CFIUS) is reportedly scrutinizing Altman’s ties to Sheikh Tahnoon. The RAND Corporation, in a recent strategic assessment, warned that the UAE’s "ambivalent" geopolitical alignment—specifically its deep-rooted tech ties to Beijing through the firm G42—could turn Stargate into a "national security backdoor" for China.

While Altman publicly advocates for AI regulation, the *New Yorker* report reveals a shadow lobbying effort designed to weaken the very rules he claims to support. Internal memos show OpenAI’s policy team was directed to push for "carve-outs" that would exempt large-scale infrastructure projects from rigorous safety audits. This dual-track strategy has left even seasoned diplomats feeling used. Former Vice President Kamala Harris, who frequently met with Altman during her time in office, was reportedly among those senior officials who expressed "significant distrust" of his motives, according to the investigation.

The lack of transparency extends to the 2024 WilmerHale investigation into Altman’s firing. While OpenAI released a sanitized summary claiming a "breakdown in trust" but no conduct mandating removal, the full written report remains under lock and key. Insiders suggest the report contains specific instances of Altman’s "lack of candor" regarding his personal investment funds and their overlap with OpenAI’s supply chain. One particularly tense section of the Sutskever memo describes a meeting where Altman, when confronted about his behavior, reportedly told the board, "I can't change my personality." Board members took this as an admission that his manipulative tactics would continue.

The social media storm following these revelations has been electric. On X.com, the tech community is fractured. "Altman is playing 4D chess with the global economy," wrote one prominent Silicon Valley analyst. "If the U.S. doesn't build Stargate with UAE money, China will build it with theirs. It's that simple." Conversely, on Reddit's r/technology, the mood is one of betrayal. "We were told this was a non-profit for the benefit of humanity," one highly upvoted comment read. "Instead, it's a vehicle for a $7 trillion power grab funded by foreign autocrats."

The stakes could not be higher. As OpenAI pushes toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), the question is no longer just about the safety of the algorithms, but the integrity of the man who controls them. With the Trump administration signaling a "trust but verify" approach to AI infrastructure, Altman’s Stargate gambit faces its greatest test. If the *New Yorker*’s findings are accurate, the "father of AGI" may have built his empire on a foundation of strategic deception, leaving the world to wonder if the technology intended to save humanity is being steered by someone primarily interested in owning it.
