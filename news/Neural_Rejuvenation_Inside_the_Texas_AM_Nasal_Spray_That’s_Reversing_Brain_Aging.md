# **Neural Rejuvenation: Inside the Texas A&M Nasal Spray That’s Reversing Brain Aging**

Silicon Valley has a new obsession, and it’s not a LLM update or a spatial computing headset. It’s a nasal spray.

While the "Blueprint" crowd has been busy tracking biomarkers and popping dozens of supplements, Dr. Ashok Shetty and his team at Texas A&M University just dropped a research paper in the *Journal of Extracellular Vesicles* that effectively changes the stack for human longevity. We’re talking about the first viable, non-invasive method to reverse brain aging by targeting the molecular root of "neuroinflammaging."

### The Tech Stack: Senolytics via Exomes
The breakthrough isn't just about what is being delivered, but *how*. The "Blood-Brain Barrier" (BBB) has long been the final boss for neuro-pharmaceuticals, blocking 98% of small molecules and nearly 100% of large ones. 

The Texas A&M solution? **Extracellular Vesicles (EVs)**—specifically, miRNA-loaded exomes derived from neural stem cells. These are biological "delivery pods" that bypass the BBB via the olfactory nerves. Once inside the hippocampus and prefrontal cortex, they deploy a payload of microRNAs designed to hunt "zombie cells"—senescent cells that have stopped dividing but refuse to die.

### Targeting the p16 and p21 Pathways
Technically speaking, the spray is a masterclass in precision medicine. It specifically targets the **p16INK4a** and **p21** pathways—the metabolic brakes that, when overexpressed, lock neurons into a state of chronic inflammation. By silencing the **NLRP3 inflammasome** and the **cGAS-STING** signaling pathways, the treatment "extinguishes" the smoldering fire of age-related inflammation.

In the 18-month-old mouse models (the equivalent of a 60-year-old human), just two doses resulted in structural rejuvenation of neural tissues and a significant uptick in spatial memory performance.
