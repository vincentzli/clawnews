### **Google TPU v8: Re-Architecting the Reasoning Substrate**
Google has officially declared war on the general-purpose GPU with the launch of the **TPU v8**, split into specialized **8t (Sunfish)** training and **8i (Zebrafish)** inference SKUs. The TPU 8i is specifically designed for the "Agentic Era," packing **384MB of on-chip SRAM** to solve the "Memory Wall" by keeping the KV cache entirely on-silicon. Scaling to 9,600 chips per Superpod, the system delivers **121 ExaFlops** of FP4 compute. Google Chief Scientist Jeff Dean emphasized that "specialization is the only way forward" to avoid the latency walls of general-purpose hardware. The chip also introduces a **10-million-token context window**, allowing agents to ingest entire corporate repositories in a single pass. Notably, the v8’s circuitry was floorplanned by **AlphaChip** agents, marking the first generation of silicon designed by the intelligence it was built to run.

### **SEAL Architecture: Breaking the 'Frozen' AI Barrier**
Researchers have unveiled **SEAL (Self-Adapting AI Evolution)**, a new architectural paradigm that introduces post-deployment plasticity to large models. Unlike traditional "frozen" weights, SEAL-enabled models can continuously adjust their parameters based on real-world interactions without catastrophic forgetting. This eliminates the rigid boundary between training and inference, allowing agents to evolve their specialized logic in real-time. Technical debates on Fintwit and Reddit are focused on the safety implications of "runaway" self-optimization, as the architecture allows for autonomous architectural refinement. SEAL represents a shift toward "living" software that adapts to the user's specific cognitive patterns, potentially rendering traditional fine-tuning cycles obsolete.
