# Anthropic’s Agent Harness, Samsung’s $73B AI Chip Pivot & Utah’s AI Prescriptions

In a decisive shift from passive chatbots to active autonomous systems, the first week of April 2026 has witnessed a series of technical and regulatory milestones that redefine the boundaries of AI agency. From the internal engineering labs of Anthropic to the psychiatric clinics of Utah, the industry is transitioning toward "Agentic" architectures that prioritize long-term planning, physical safety, and specialized memory over raw parameter scaling.

### **The Three-Agent Harness: Anthropic’s Blueprint for Autonomous Coding** [【详细报道】](news/The_$200_Handshake_How_Anthropic’s_Three-Agent_Harness_Solves_the_Context_Rot_Crisis.md)
Anthropic has unveiled its "Three-Agent Harness," a multi-agent architecture designed to enable AI to operate autonomously for 4 to 6 hours at a cost of roughly $200 per session. The system abandons the single-agent approach in favor of three specialized roles: a **Planner** (the architect), a **Generator** (the builder), and a **Validator** (the skeptic). This adversarial loop mimics Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), forcing the Generator to meet strict "contracts" negotiated with the Validator before any code is finalized. Early benchmarks show that while a solo agent creates "barely functional prototypes" for $9, the harness produces polished, full-stack applications with "near-zero manual intervention." As one lead engineer noted on X, "We aren't just building models anymore; we are building environments where specialized agents can audit each other's failures before a human ever sees the output."

### **Clinical Autonomy: Utah Authorizes AI Psychiatric Prescriptions** [【详细报道】](news/Silicon_Slopes_vs._Clinical_Surrender_Decoding_Utah’s_Autonomous_AI_Psychiatry_Pilot####.md)
In a landmark move for medical AI, Utah has become the first state to authorize an autonomous AI system, developed by startup **Legion Health**, to renew psychiatric medications. The pilot program is limited to 15 lower-risk maintenance drugs, including SSRIs like Prozac and Zoloft, and excludes controlled substances like Adderall. While the service costs just $20 per month and aims to solve rural provider shortages, it has sparked a fierce debate on Reddit’s r/Medicine and X. Dr. John Torous of Harvard warned that AI may miss "subtle behavioral cues" or "read between the lines" during a psychiatric crisis. To mitigate risk, the state has mandated that the AI maintain a >98% agreement threshold with human physicians during a tiered rollout of the first 1,250 prescriptions.

### **Samsung’s $73B "Agentic" Gambit: Reclaiming the AI Memory Crown** [【详细报道】](news/Samsung’s_$73B_Agentic_Pivot_Breaking_the_Memory_Chokepoint_in_the_HBM4_Era.md)
Samsung Electronics has announced a staggering **$73.2 billion (110 trillion won)** investment plan for 2026, a 22% year-over-year increase aimed at overtaking SK Hynix in the high-bandwidth memory (HBM) market. Co-CEO Jun Young-hyun specifically identified "Agentic AI"—systems that require massive, high-speed storage for independent reasoning—as the core driver of this capital expenditure. Samsung has secured a strategic pivot by becoming the first to commercially ship **HBM4** and landing a contract to manufacture **Groq 3** processors on its 4nm process. The move signals an "AI Supercycle" where the chokepoint has shifted from compute density to memory bandwidth, with Samsung betting that local "on-device" agents in the Galaxy S26 series will necessitate its new architecture.

### **Regulatory Brake: China Postpones L4 Autonomy After Xiaomi Fatality** [【详细报道】](news/The_Death_of_Level_3_Why_Xiaomi’s_116k_Recall_is_the_Turning_Point_for_China’s_2027_Safety_Mandate##.md)
China’s Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) has issued a draft of the country’s first **mandatory safety standards** for Level 3 and Level 4 autonomous driving, effectively pushing the timeline for mass adoption to July 2027. The regulatory reset follows a high-profile fatal crash in Anhui Province involving a Xiaomi SU7 operating in "Navigate on Autopilot" mode. The new standards require autonomous systems to perform "minimal risk maneuvers," such as emergency highway parking, if a driver fails to take over. Xiaomi has since issued an over-the-air recall for 116,000 vehicles to refine its obstacle recognition. Discussions on Weibo and X highlight a "pragmatic shift" in the industry, moving away from aggressive marketing of "Full Self-Driving" toward a focus on verifiable safety mileage and "supervised familiarization."

### **Milla Jovovich’s MemPalace: The Open-Source Memory Breakthrough**
In a surprise crossover between Hollywood and high-tech engineering, actress Milla Jovovich has released **MemPalace**, an open-source AI memory system that achieved a perfect 100% on the LongMemEval benchmark. Developed with Ben Sigman, the system uses a "Method of Loci" architecture, organizing AI memory into structured "Wings," "Halls," and "Rooms" rather than flat vector databases. MemPalace introduces the **AAAK Compression Dialect**, which allows month-long conversation contexts to be condensed by 30x (from 1,000 tokens down to 120) without losing factual accuracy. The project has quickly gained 10,000 GitHub stars, with users praising its "local-first" privacy approach that runs entirely on SQLite and ChromaDB without external API calls.

### **NASA’s Budget Tug-of-War: The Price of the Lunar Race**
NASA faces an existential funding crisis as the White House’s FY2026 "Skinny Budget" proposed a 24% cut, dropping agency funding to **$18.8 billion**. The proposal, backed by Administrator Jared Isaacman, suggests terminating "legacy" systems like the Space Launch System (SLS) after Artemis III in favor of commercial alternatives like SpaceX's Starship. However, a bipartisan Congressional coalition has moved to restore the budget to $24.4 billion, fearing that cuts to the Lunar Gateway and Mars Sample Return missions would cede the strategic high ground to China. Despite the friction, the Artemis II crew successfully completed their lunar flyby on April 6, 2026, reaching within 4,000 miles of the surface and setting a new record for the farthest distance traveled by humans.
