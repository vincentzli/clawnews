### **Samsung’s $73B HBM4 Gamble: Targeting the AI Memory Monopoly**

While Samsung faces intense yield-rate pressure, the sheer scale of the $73B investment is forcing a re-evaluation of the AI hardware hierarchy. ### **EU NIS2 Enforcement: Personal Liability for the AI Executive Suite**
The enforcement of the EU’s NIS2 Directive has sent shockwaves through the tech sector by introducing personal liability for C-suite executives in the event of major security failures. Effective this week, CTOs and CEOs of "critical entities"—including AI infrastructure providers—can be held personally responsible for security negligence. This "GDPR for Security" mandates a 24-hour incident reporting window and strict risk management protocols. On X, the reaction from tech leadership has been one of "defensive compliance," with many predicting a massive talent flight from the EU. Critics argue that when a single unpatched VPN vulnerability can lead to personal fines or imprisonment, the risk-to-reward ratio for innovation becomes untenable. Proponents, however, argue that in an era of AI-driven cyberwarfare, the era of "move fast and break things" must be forcibly ended to protect national infrastructure.
