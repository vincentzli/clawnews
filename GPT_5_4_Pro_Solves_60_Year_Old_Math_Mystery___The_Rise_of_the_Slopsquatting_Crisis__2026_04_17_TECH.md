### **The "Slopsquatting" Security Crisis: AI Hallucinations as an Attack Vector**
[Read More](news/Slopsquatting_The_0_Attack_Vector_Exploiting_the_Vibe_Coding_Security_Gap.md)
A new software supply chain threat known as "slopsquatting" has reached a critical level this week. Security researchers report that attackers are now systematically identifying non-existent software packages frequently hallucinated by LLMs—such as `fastapi-middleware` or `aws-helper-sdk`—and registering them on PyPI and npm with malicious payloads. A recent analysis found that 58% of hallucinated packages are predictable and reappear across different sessions, allowing hackers to "squat" on these phantom dependencies. With the rise of "vibe coding" in 2026, where developers rapidly accept AI code without verification, the vulnerability rate for AI-generated code has spiked to 2.74 times higher than human-written code. Cybersecurity figures on X.com are calling for a "Zero-Trust AI" architecture to mandate dependency verification for every AI-suggested import.
