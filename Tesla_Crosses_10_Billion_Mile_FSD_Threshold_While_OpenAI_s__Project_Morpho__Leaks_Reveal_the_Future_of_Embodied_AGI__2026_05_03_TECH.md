# Tesla Crosses 10 Billion Mile FSD Threshold While OpenAI’s "Project Morpho" Leaks Reveal the Future of Embodied AGI

### **Tesla Hits 10 Billion Mile FSD Milestone and the HW3 "Autonomy Gap"** [Read More](news/The_HW3_Sunset_Why_10_Billion_Miles_Couldn't_Save_Tesla’s_Legacy_Silicon.md)
On May 3, 2026, Tesla officially announced that its Full Self-Driving (Supervised) fleet has surpassed 10 billion cumulative miles driven, a data milestone that Elon Musk has long cited as the "critical mass" for transitioning to unsupervised Level 4 autonomy. "We now have more real-world corner-case data than every other AV company combined by an order of magnitude," Musk posted on X.com earlier today. However, the celebration is overshadowed by a massive hardware schism. Internal leaks and recent fleet updates confirm that the upcoming FSD v15—a 10-billion-parameter neural network—will be physically incompatible with Hardware 3 (HW3). Tesla is reportedly quietly rolling out a "Legacy Support" program for HW3 owners, offering a $2,500 trade-in credit toward AI4-equipped models, sparking a firestorm on Reddit’s r/TeslaMotors. "I paid $12k for a promise that my 2022 Model 3 would be a robotaxi. Now I’m being told my hardware is a paperweight for the AGI era," wrote one user in a thread with over 45,000 upvotes.

### **OpenAI’s "Project Morpho" Leaks: The End of the Chatbot Era** [Read More](news/Beyond_the_Chatbox_Inside_Project_Morpho_and_the_Dawn_of_Unified_Embodiment.md)
Leaked benchmarks and architectural diagrams for OpenAI’s "Project Morpho" have surfaced on GitHub and X, suggesting a radical departure from the text-centric Orion models covered last month. Morpho is reportedly a Vision-Language-Action (VLA) foundation model designed specifically for "Embodied AGI." Unlike Orion, which focused on persistent memory and 10-million-token context windows, Morpho is built for real-time sensory stream processing, allowing it to "morph" its reasoning across different robotic bodies. Notable AI researcher Andrej Karpathy commented on the leak, stating, "The Move from LLM to VLA is the most significant architectural shift since the Transformer. We are moving from AI that talks about the world to AI that inhabits it." Morpho reportedly achieves a 98% success rate on "zero-shot" physical tasks, such as autonomously navigating a chaotic kitchen to prepare a meal, using standard off-the-shelf humanoid hardware.

### **Neuralink "Blindsight" UAE Trials: Restoring 20/20 Vision at Scale** [Read More](news/The_Dubai_Protocol_Inside_Neuralink’s_2026_Pivot_to_Full-Spectrum_Vision_and_the_3,000-Electrode_Frontier.md)
Neuralink confirmed this morning that its first 100-patient cohort for the "Blindsight" clinical trials has been fully selected in Dubai, in partnership with the Cleveland Clinic Abu Dhabi. This marks the transition from experimental single-patient proof-of-concepts to high-volume medical production. The 2026 "Blindsight" implant features an upgraded 3,000-electrode array, double the density of the 2025 prototype. Preliminary data from the "Patient Zero" graduation ceremony shows that the participant achieved 20/20 visual acuity in the center of the field, with the ability to toggle into an "Enhanced Mode" for infrared spectrum viewing. "Seeing the Dubai skyline in 10-bit color was something I was told was biologically impossible," the patient stated during a live-streamed press conference. Neuralink’s R1 surgical robot has now reached a 15-minute "incision-to-implant" cycle time, paving the way for what Musk calls the "democratization of the sensory experience."

### **Figure 03 "Helix" Mass Production: The $20,000 Humanoid Reality** [Read More](news/The_Helix_Epoch_Inside_Figure_03’s_20K_Mass_Production_and_the_End_of_the_OpenAI_Era.md)
Figure AI has officially scaled its "BotQ" facility in California to a production rate of one Figure 03 robot per hour. Running on the new proprietary "Helix" VLA model—which completely replaced the former OpenAI-integrated stack in early 2026—the Figure 03 has begun limited deployments in consumer homes for beta testing. The robot’s "Vulcan" AI policy provides what engineers call "Superhuman Robustness," allowing the 55kg humanoid to maintain balance and task focus even after losing functionality in multiple joints. "The Figure 03 isn't a gadget; it's the first consumer-grade labor appliance," said Figure CEO Brett Adcock. At a target price point of $20,000, the robot is currently being debated on X and LinkedIn as the primary disruptor of the 2027 blue-collar labor market, with the first 10,000 units already reserved by logistics and elder-care firms.

### **Microsoft Stargate Phase 4 "Power-On": 1GW for the Next Frontier**
Microsoft and OpenAI have officially "powered on" Phase 4 of the Stargate supercomputer initiative. Located in a specialized "Nuclear-AI Zone" in the Pacific Northwest, this phase draws a continuous 1.2 gigawatts of power, primarily sourced from a dedicated fleet of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs). The facility is reportedly being used to train "Orion-2," the first model built on the Vera Rubin architecture. Energy analysts on Fintwit (Financial Twitter) are flagging the massive 10GW target for Phase 5 (scheduled for 2028) as the primary driver for the current "Nuclear Renaissance," as AI demand now accounts for 15% of total U.S. grid load.

### **Waymo London Pilot: Level 4 Robotaxis Cross the Atlantic**
Waymo has officially launched its first European pilot program in London this week. Using a fleet of modified, right-hand-drive Jaguar I-PACEs, the service is currently operating in a "Small-Scale Pilot" mode within the Greater London area. Following the UK’s 2025 "Autonomous Vehicle Act," Waymo is the first non-European company to receive a Level 4 permit for zero-occupant testing. Competitor Wayve is reportedly feeling the pressure, as Waymo’s "Sixth-Generation Sensor Suite" has shown 99.9% reliability in London’s notoriously difficult rainy and foggy conditions. "The fog isn't a bug for Waymo; it's just another feature of the training set," posted a lead engineer from Wayve on r/SelfDrivingCars.

### **California Legalizes Heavy-Duty Autonomous Freight**
In a landmark regulatory shift, California has officially legalized driverless heavy-duty trucks (over 10,000 lbs) on state highways. This follows the successful "Route 66" coast-to-coast unmanned haul completed by a convoy of Kodiak and Gatik trucks earlier this month. The new regulations require a "Remote Command Center" override but no longer mandate a physical human in the sleeper cab. Union groups have already filed petitions on X, warning that 250,000 long-haul jobs could be automated by 2030, while logistics firms cite a 40% reduction in fuel and insurance costs as a "necessary evolution" for the global supply chain.
