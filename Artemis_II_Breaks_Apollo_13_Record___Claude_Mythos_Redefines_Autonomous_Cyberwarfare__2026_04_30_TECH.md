### **Artemis II: Humanity Shatters the All-Time Distance Record from Earth**
On April 6, 2026, the crew of the Artemis II mission officially surpassed the record for the farthest distance humans have ever traveled from Earth, previously held for 56 years by the crew of Apollo 13. The Orion spacecraft, named *Integrity*, reached a maximum distance of **252,756 miles (406,771 km)** as it looped around the far side of the Moon. Commander Reid Wiseman, Pilot Victor Glover, and Mission Specialists Christina Koch and Jeremy Hansen passed within 4,067 miles of the lunar surface, capturing unprecedented high-resolution imagery of the South Pole-Aitken basin. "We are seeing the cradle of the lunar future," Glover remarked during the live broadcast. ### **GDDRHammer: New Hardware Exploit Pierces NVIDIA’s GPU Security**
Researchers have disclosed a critical hardware-level vulnerability titled **GDDRHammer**, which targets NVIDIA’s Ampere and Ada Lovelace architectures utilizing GDDR6 memory. By "hammering" specific physical memory rows on an RTX 3060 or RTX 6000 Ada, attackers can induce bit flips (over **1,100 flips** recorded in some tests) to corrupt GPU Page Tables. This allows an unprivileged user to bypass the GPU’s isolation and gain **arbitrary read/write access** to the host CPU's system RAM via Direct Memory Access (DMA). The vulnerability is particularly devastating for multi-tenant AI cloud providers where multiple customers share physical GPU hardware. NVIDIA has issued an urgent advisory recommending the immediate activation of ECC (Error Correction Code) memory and strict IOMMU configurations to prevent "GPU-to-CPU" privilege escalation.
