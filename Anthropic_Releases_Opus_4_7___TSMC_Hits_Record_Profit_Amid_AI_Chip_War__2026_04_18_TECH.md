### **TSMC vs. Samsung: The Battle for 2nm Sovereignty and Tesla's AI6**
[Read More](news/Untitled_3.md)
The semiconductor landscape shifted dramatically this week as **TSMC** reported a record Q1 2026 operating profit of **NT$658.9 billion ($20.4 billion)**, a 61.9% year-on-year surge. This growth is driven by what CEO C.C. Wei described as "insatiable" demand for AI accelerators from NVIDIA and Apple. Despite this win, industry analysts on X point to a widening yield gap: TSMC’s 2nm process is reportedly hitting **60-70% yields**, while Samsung is struggling to maintain **55%**. Intel Foundry has also emerged as a potent third player, claiming the spot of the world's second-largest foundry by revenue ($4.5 billion in the latest quarter) and positioning its **14A process** as the ultimate challenger to TSMC's dominance in 2027.

While Samsung emphasizes "privacy-first" processing, the **Mozilla Foundation** recently issued a warning regarding the data retention policies of "contextual home AI."
